# Market Research — Faceless Digital Products for Moms (CaroTech), June 2026

*Live web scan. Directional, not exact — validate by listing and watching sales.*

## Summary
The faceless digital-products market for moms is healthy and growing in 2026.
The winning play has shifted from single cheap files to **bundles/"vaults"** sold
on Gumroad/Etsy, paired with **value-first, faceless content** (Pinterest + IG +
TikTok) driving traffic to a low-priced entry guide that upsells into bundles —
exactly CaroTech's funnel ($17 guide → bump → resell upsell).

## Key opportunities
- **Bundle, don't single-sell.** A "Complete Home Management Binder" (50 pages,
  $12) beats a $3 grocery list. On Gumroad, "vaults" of 5 related products at
  $27 outperform a single $7 planner.
- **Lean into 2026 parenting trends** (Pinterest's first Parenting Report):
  intentional/"slow" parenting, **screen-free activities (+200% YoY)**,
  "no phone summer" (+340%), digital-detox, family traditions, road-trip & travel
  printables, sensory/life-skills activities for kids.
- **AI + faceless angle is on-trend** and matches CaroTech's promise (start
  without showing your face). "How to start" beginner content converts well.
- **Coherent shop > scattered.** Start with 5–10 tightly related products in ONE
  sub-niche; a clear theme converts far better than mixed topics.

## Pricing benchmarks (2026)
- **Etsy:** $7–$27 per item/bundle
- **Gumroad:** $17–$47 (flat 10% fee, no monthly cost)
- **Digital guides:** $27–$97
- CaroTech's **$17 entry guide is well-placed**; add a $27–$47 bundle upsell.

## Income context (realistic, not guaranteed)
- 50–100 optimized listings → ~$500–$2,000/mo is a realistic target
- 300–500 listings in demand categories → $3,000–$8,000/mo for established shops
- Treat these as ceilings that take time + volume, not quick wins.
