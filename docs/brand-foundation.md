## Who it's for (ideal client)

- **Company stage:** Seed to Series B B2B SaaS ($1M–$30M ARR). Big enough to have budget, small enough to lack an in-house copy team.
- **Buyer:** Head of Growth, Head of Marketing, or a founder still wearing the marketing hat.
- **Pain:** Trials don't convert, onboarding emails go unread, the homepage "explains the product" instead of selling the outcome.

## What we sell (the outcome, not the deliverable)

Clients don't buy "words." They buy:
- More trial→paid conversions
- Lower churn through better onboarding/lifecycle email
- Landing pages that beat the control in an A/B test
- A homepage that finally explains *why* in 5 seconds

Always lead with the metric. The deliverable (the email, the page) is just how we get there.

## Brand voice

**Personality:** Sharp, plain-spoken, confident, a little contrarian. The opposite of "synergy" marketing-speak.

**Do:**
- Short sentences. Concrete nouns. Active verbs.
- Lead with the reader's problem, not our process.
- Use specifics and numbers ("cut onboarding emails from 6 to 3, lifted activation 18%").
- Sound like a smart person talking, not a brochure.

**Don't:**
- "Unlock," "leverage," "seamless," "world-class," "passionate about."
- Adjective stacks ("innovative, cutting-edge, dynamic solutions").
- Hedge. Say the thing.

**Tagline options:**
- *Words that move users to act.* ✅ (primary)
- *Copy that converts. SaaS that grows.*
- *We write to a metric, not a word count.*
