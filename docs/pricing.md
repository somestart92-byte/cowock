> Principle: price the **value**, not the hours. SaaS-only positioning supports premium rates. These are starting numbers — raise them after every 2–3 closed clients.

## Monthly retainers (the goal)

Recurring copy across email, pages, and in-app. **This is where the business becomes stable.**

| Retainer | Roughly includes | Price/mo |
|----------|-------------------|----------|
| **Lite** | ~1 major deliverable/mo (e.g. one sequence or page) + light edits | **$1,500** |
| **Growth** ⭐ | ~2–3 deliverables/mo + ongoing edits + monthly strategy call | **$2,500** |
| **Partner** | High-volume / multi-surface, priority turnaround, embedded in the team | **$4,500+** |

Retainers are billed monthly in advance. 3-month minimum recommended to allow results to show.

## The pilot offer (for cold prospects)

To de-risk the first engagement with a skeptical buyer:

> **Conversion Pilot — $750.** One landing-page section or a 3-email mini-sequence, fully researched, delivered in 5 business days. If you don't want to keep working together, we part as friends. If you do, the $750 credits toward your first project or month of retainer.

Use this to convert "I'm not sure" into a paid first step.

---

## Rules of thumb

- **Always present 3 tiers.** Most buyers choose the middle. The top tier makes the middle look reasonable.
- **Quote per project, never per hour.** Hourly punishes you for getting faster.
- **50% deposit** to start any project; balance on delivery.
- **Raise rates** every 2–3 closed clients — your portfolio is the justification.
- **Anchor on ROI:** "One extra trial-to-paid conversion a month often pays for the whole retainer." Make that math explicit to the buyer.
