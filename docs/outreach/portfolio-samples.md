# Portfolio Samples (Spec Work)

You don't need real clients to have a portfolio. Write **spec samples** for real or fictional products across different industries — it shows you can write for any business. These go on the site and get attached to pitches.

For each sample, show **the strategy, not just the words** — and demonstrate honest persuasion (real benefits, real proof, no fake urgency).

---

## Sample 1 — Trial onboarding email (SaaS, fictional "Tasklane")

**Context note (include this above the copy):**
> *Goal: get new trial users to create their first project within 24 hours — the activation moment that predicts paid conversion. Research: mined 40 reviews of similar tools; the #1 frustration was "I signed up and didn't know where to start."*

**Subject:** Your first project takes 90 seconds (here's how)

> Hi [First name],
>
> Most people who try Tasklane stall on the same thing: a blank dashboard.
>
> So let's skip that. Create your first project right now — it takes about 90 seconds, and it's the moment Tasklane actually starts saving you time:
>
> **→ Create your first project**
>
> Don't overthink it. Use this week's real work. You can change everything later.
>
> Reply if you get stuck — a real person (me) reads these.
>
> — Sam, Tasklane

**Why it works:** Names the exact drop-off point, gives one action (not five), removes the fear of "doing it wrong," opens a reply loop. No hype.

## Sample 2 — Landing page hero rewrite (e-commerce / any brand)

**Before (typical):**
> *"An innovative, all-in-one platform that leverages cutting-edge technology to streamline your life."*

**After:**
> ## Headline
> **Choices you don't have to second-guess.**
>
> ## Subhead
> Everything vetted, in one place — so you decide in seconds instead of spending an evening reading the fine print.
>
> ## CTA
> Get started — free

**Why it works:** Leads with the real emotional outcome (peace of mind, less effort), is concrete, drops the empty "innovative/all-in-one" stack. Honest and specific.

## Sample 3 — Re-engagement email (inactive user, any business)

**Subject:** should I close your account?

> Hi [First name],
>
> You started with [Company] a couple weeks back and then things went quiet — which usually means one of two things:
>
> 1. It wasn't the right fit (totally fine — reply "close it" and I'll tidy up).
> 2. You got busy and never finished setting up.
>
> If it's #2, I'll walk you through it in a 10-minute screen share. Just reply "help."
>
> Either way, I'd rather hear from you than keep emailing into the void.
>
> — Sam

**Why it works:** Pattern-interrupt subject line, gives a genuine easy out (which builds trust), turns re-engagement into a human offer instead of a manipulative discount.

## How to build your real portfolio fast

1. **Pick 2–3 products you actually use** (in different industries). Sign up, screenshot the real emails/pages.
2. **Rewrite one piece each** with a before/after and a strategy note.
3. **Publish them** — on the site (`site/`), as LinkedIn carousels, or a linkable Google Doc/Notion page.
4. **Use them as your cold-email "free deliverable"** — send the rewrite to the actual company.
5. Replace spec work with **real client results** the moment you have them (metrics > samples).
